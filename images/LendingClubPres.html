Ryan Kuhn

What is Lending Club?

  • Peer-to-Peer lending site where investors buy and trade shares of personal loans.
  • Like the bond market, investors earn a return when the borrower pays interest. The borrowers get access to lower cost credit by cutting out banks in the middle. According to Lending Club, on average borrows get a 33% lower interest rate than they were paying on other loans.
  • Interest rates can go as high as from 25%.
  • Diversify your portfolio with an asset class that is not tied to the stock market.

www.lendingclub.com

Why Use the Lending Club Package?

Save time

Stop sifting through loans manually. Using the LendingClub package, you can automate your purchases so they happen quickly and easily.

Put your strategy on cruise control

Code up your investment strategy and then walk away. Use the LendingClub package to prevent idle cash and be sure your account is working hard for you.

Use the Power of R

R comes with the analytical tools to create a complex and robust investment strategy. Leverage that power to ensure you maximize your returns as an investor.


What does the software do?

The LendingClub package connects your R environment to the Lending Club API. It allows you to connect directly to their servers without going through the website. Search for new loans and then buy them, sell loans you already own, or transfer money into or our of your account.

Start by pulling the loan listing and use R’s rich ecosystem of analytics and machine learning libraries to make data driven decisions about which loans to invest in. Then use you LendingClub to make your trades in real time.

Download the Package

The software is hosted on GitHub and on CRAN. You can install the software by typing this in the R console:

install.packages("LendingClub")
library(LendingClub)

Or install the latest development version:

devtools::install_github("kuhnrl30/LendingClub")
library(LendingClub)

Example Use Case

  1. Download the list of loans currently for sale.
  2. Filter the loans base on the criteria important to you. Advanced users may use machine learning or other analytics to score and rank the loans.
  3. Use the API to purchase several loans quickly before they are bought by other investors.
library(LendingClub)
library(dplyr)

Loans<- ListedLoans()$content$loans

# Filter to loans with yields greather than 15% and discounted 
# from face value
Sorted_Loans<- Loans %>%
    filter(YTM > 15, Markup.Discont > 0) %>%  
    arrange(desc(YTM))

SubmitOrder(loanId= SortedLoans$id[1],
            amount= 25)
